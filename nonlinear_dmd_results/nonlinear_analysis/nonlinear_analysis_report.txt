PROBLEM DESCRIPTION:
----------------------------------------
Nonlinear Diffusion Equation: ∂u/∂t = ∂/∂x[D(x) * ∂u/∂x] + S(x)
where D(x) = ν_min + ν₀ * u² (nonlinear diffusion coefficient)
ν_min = 1e-4, ν₀ = 1e-2

METHODS COMPARED:
----------------------------------------
1. Standard DMD: Augmented state [u; S; 1]
2. Extended Physics-Informed DMD: Augmented state [u; S; 1; z]
   where z = ∂/∂x[D(x) * ∂u/∂x] (nonlinear term)

CONCLUSIONS:
----------------------------------------
The Extended Physics-Informed DMD approach successfully addresses the
challenge of modeling nonlinear PDEs with DMD. By explicitly including
the nonlinear physics in the augmented state vector, we can maintain
the computational efficiency of DMD while capturing the essential
nonlinear dynamics. This represents a significant advancement over
standard DMD for nonlinear systems.
